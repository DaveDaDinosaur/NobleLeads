Primary Combinations

  • Gold #C8A45A on True Black #0A0A0A — hero text, CTAs
  • Cream #F5EDD8 on Charcoal #141414 — body copy
  • Black #0A0A0A on Gold #C8A45A — filled buttons
  • Gold #C8A45A on Dark #1C1C1C — card accents
  • Cream Pale #FAF6EE on Black — contrast sections

Usage Rules

  • Gold is ALWAYS an accent — never a full background for large areas
  • Never use pure #FFFFFF — always warm white or cream
  • Dark backgrounds should dominate (70–80% of any design)
  • Gold should never appear on Gold Light — no light-on-light
  • Charcoal / Dark / Black can be mixed freely for depth

07 — Imagery Direction
Visual Language
"Dark, dramatic, purposeful — the craftsman behind the brand."
✦ Use This
  • Dark / moody photography — dramatic lighting, deep contrast
  • Close-up craft shots: tools, materials, finished work
  • Before/after with side-by-side layout in dark frame
  • Overlaid with translucent black + subtle gold text
  • Clean, uncluttered compositions with deliberate negative space
  • Real tradespeople — not stock-photo-perfect strangers
  • Gold gradient overlays on image-heavy posts
Avoid: Generic stock photos, rainbow gradients, white backgrounds with no atmosphere.
✗ Avoid This
  • Bright white / pastel backgrounds
  • Generic stock photos of people in suits
  • Rainbow or purple gradient overlays
  • Cluttered layouts with too many elements
  • Comic Sans or any novelty fonts
  • Bright, saturated colours beyond gold
  • Red, blue, or green accent colours
08 — Brand Voice
Copy Direction

✦ Write Like This

✓"More calls. More booked jobs. Less chasing."
✓"You're great at the work. Getting clients shouldn't be this hard."
✓"Stop paying Checkatrade to rent your own customers."
✓"A lead machine you own — not rent."
✓Short sentences. Plain English. Outcome first.
✓UK spelling: "enquiries", "recognised", "optimised"

✗ Don't Write Like This

✗"Leveraging cutting-edge omnichannel marketing solutions..."
✗"Synergising your brand's digital footprint across touchpoints"
✗"We are passionate about helping your business thrive!"
✗American spellings: "optimize", "analyze"
✗Excessive exclamation marks or emojis in professional content
✗Vague promises without concrete outcomes
